How it works

Three moves, then it disappears

No new window to talk to. No paste step. You never leave what you were doing.

1

Hold your hotkey

Press and hold your push-to-talk key from any app. A small, non-intrusive indicator shows Undertone is listening — it never steals your cursor.

2

Say what you mean

Speak naturally — ramble, restart, think out loud. Undertone transcribes as you talk, so the moment you release there is almost nothing left to wait for.

3

Release — it's typed

Let go and polished text appears at your cursor, matched to the app you're in. A chat reply reads like chat; a commit message reads like code.

The difference

Raw dictation dumps words. Undertone writes.

Most voice tools hand you exactly what the microphone heard. Undertone closes the gap between that and the sentence you actually meant — the same words, minus the mess.

What the mic heard

um so hey can you send me the the deck by end of day no wait scratch that send it by tomorrow morning i want to review it first comma thanks

What Undertone types

Hey — can you send me the deck by tomorrow morning? I want to review it first. Thanks.

Filler and false starts dropped, the stutter cleaned up, "scratch that" obeyed, "comma" turned into punctuation — and the tone set for a quick message.

Privacy is the product

Your words are yours. We built it that way.

A dictation tool hears everything you write. That is exactly why privacy is a design constraint here, not a policy footnote — the architecture makes these promises keepable.

Audio is never stored

Your audio is transcribed in real time and discarded — it never touches our disks.

History encrypted at rest

History is encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM; plaintext never hits our storage.

Telemetry without your words

Usage analytics count words and measure latency. They never carry what you said.
